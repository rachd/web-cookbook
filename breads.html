Banana Bread

Makes One Loaf

Ingredients

  • 3 over-ripe bananas
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 eggs, beaten until light
  • 2 cups flour
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup chopped coarsely walnuts

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Mix together bananas, butter, and eggs
  3. In a separate bowl, sift together flour, sugar, salt, baking soda, cinnamon, allspice, and nutmeg.
  4. Stir the dry ingredients and the walnuts into the wet ingredients.
  5. Pour the mixture into a buttered 9x5 loaf pan.
  6. Bake for 1 hour.
  7. Remove the loaf from the pan and allow it to cool on its side before cutting.

Banana Muffins

Makes 12 muffins

from mymommystyle.com

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large bananas, mashed
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1/3 cup butter, melted

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Coat muffin pans with non-stick spray, or use paper liners.
  3. Sift together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt; set aside.
  4. Combine bananas, sugar, egg, vanilla and melted butter in a large bowl.
  5. Fold in flour mixture, and mix until dry ingredients are just combined. Do not over mix.
  6. Scoop about 1/4 cup of batter each muffin tin.
  7. Bake in preheated oven for 15-20 minutes. Muffins will spring back when lightly tapped.

Blueberry Muffins

Makes 12 muffins

from seriouseats.com

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces all-purpose flour (2 1/3 cups)
  • 5 1/4 ounces sugar (3/4 cup)
  • 2 teaspoons (8 grams) baking powder
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt (for table salt use half as much by volume or use same weight)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander seed
  • 1/8 teaspoon grated nutmeg
  • 6 ounces unsalted butter (1 1/2 sticks) cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 4 ounces milk (1/2 cup)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 12 ounces fresh blueberries (2 cups)
  • optional: 2 ounces sparkling sugar (1/3 cup)

Directions

  1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat to 350 degrees
  2. Prepare a nonstick or aluminum muffin pan by greasing lightly or lining with tinfoil or paper liners
  3. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, baking soda, coriander, nutmeg, and butter in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment
  4. Mix at low speed to form a mealy powder, about 2 minutes
  5. Add milk, eggs, and vanilla (if using), mixing briefly to form a thick batter
  6. Drop a heaping tablespoonful (about 1 ounce, or 28g if you want to be mathematically precise) of plain dough into the bottom of each muffin cup
  7. Fold blueberries into remaining dough, which will be extremely stiff
  8. Divide remaining dough evenly between each cup
  9. If you like, top each muffin with sparkling sugar, about 1 teaspoon each or to taste
  10. Bake until muffins are puffed and firm, about 25 minutes
  11. Enjoy immediately and store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 1 day

Challah

Makes one loaf

from finecooking.com

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 16 3/4 ounces (3 1/2 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • sesame or poppy seeds for topping (optional)

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, mix yeast with 1/2 cup of flour
  2. Stir in warm water and let set for 15-20 minutes
  3. Stir in 3 eggs, oil, honey, and salt until well-combined but lumpy
  4. Add in remaining flour, mixing until combined
  5. Knead until smooth, about 2 minutes
  6. Transfer dough to large bowl, cover, and let rise for 2 hours
  7. Line insulated baking sheet (or two stacked) with parchment or oiled foil
  8. Turn dough out on lightly floured work surface, sprinkling with more flour
  9. Spread and flatten dough but don't punch it down
  10. Cut dough into 6 equal pieces
  11. Roll each piece into thin sheet, between 1/8 and 1/4 inch thick
  12. Roll up each piece into a strand, snaking between hands until 12-15 inches long
  13. Braid strands together: 5 -> 1, 6 -> 4, 2 -> 6, 1 -> 3
  14. Transfer braid to baking sheet and cover loosely with plastic wrap
  15. Let braid rise for 2 hours, until it remains indented when lightly pressed
  16. Preheat over to 325 degrees and position oven rack in lower third
  17. Just before baking, brush dough with beaten eggs and topping
  18. Poke bread all over with skewer
  19. Bake for 20 minutes, rotate 180 degrees, then bake 15 minutes
  20. If bread browns too rapidly, cover with foil

Peach Focaccia

from alwaysorderdessert.com

Ingredients

  • 2 cups warm water
  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 large peaches, pitted and diced but not peeled
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup brandy or bourbon
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme

Directions

  1. Whisk together the water, yeast, and sugar in mixer bowl. Let sit five minutes until foamy.
  2. Add flour, cinnamon, allspice, salt, and olive oil, mixing with a dough hook for 1-2 minutes
  3. Kneed for 5 minutes (dough should be sticky)
  4. Grease a bowl with olive oil
  5. Scrape dough into bowl, turning once to coat
  6. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and let dough rise for one hour
  7. While dough rises, combine peaches, lemon juice, brown sugar, vanilla, brandy, and thyme
  8. Preheat oven to 425 degrees
  9. Coat cookie sheet pan with olive oil
  10. Dump dough onto pan, spreading it out to the edges
  11. Dimple the dough and let rise until doubled (30-40 minutes)
  12. Arrange the peach mixture over the dough, pressing in slightly
  13. Bake for 25 minutes

Peach Muffins

from food.com

Makes 12 muffins

Note: oily

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 light brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 eggs, mixed
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 1/4 cups chopped peaches
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds (optional)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Lightly grease muffin tins
  3. In large mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, baking soda, sugars, and cinnamon
  4. Make a well in the center and add eggs, oil, and vanilla, stirring until moistened
  5. Stir in almonds and peaches
  6. Pour about 1/3 cup of batter into each muffin tin
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes

Pretzels

from sophisticatedgourmet.com

Makes 12 pretzels

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/8 teaspoon fine grain sea salt
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 1 cup warm water (100 - 110 degrees)
  • 1 cup (128 grams) bread flour
  • 2 cups (256 grams) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons salted butter, softened
  • vegetable oil for bowl and baking sheets
  • 1/4 cup baking soda
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon pretzel salt or coarse grain sea salt
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon water

Directions

  1. Mix yeast, sea salt, 2 tsp sugar, and warm water in small bowl, whisking until sugar dissolves
  2. Let stand until foamy, about 5 to 10 minutes
  3. Place flour into a large bowl
  4. Cut butter into flour until mixture resembles coarse crumbs
  5. Slowly pour yeast mixture over flour mixture, stirring with wooden spoon to combine
  6. Using your hands, gather dough together
  7. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and kneed until no longer sticky, about 5 minutes
  8. Lightly brush a large bowl with oil and turn the dought to coat
  9. Cover with plastic and let rise in a warm place until dough has doubled in size, about 1 hour
  10. Cut dough into 12 equal pieces and roll each into an 18-inch rope
  11. Form a U shape with 1 rope and twist ends together twice
  12. Fold twisted portions backward along center of U shape to form a circle, then gently press ends of rope onto dough to seal
  13. Transfer to an oiled baking sheet and repeat with rest of dough
  14. Let rise for 20 minutes
  15. Preheat oven to 475 degrees
  16. Bring a large pot of water to a boil
  17. Add baking soda and 1 1/2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  18. Boil pretzels in batches until puffed and shiny, about 30 seconds a side
  19. Transfer to wire racks to drain
  20. Place pretzels on an oiled baking sheet
  21. Mix the 1 teaspoon water and egg in a small bowl
  22. Brush each pretzel with the egg wash
  23. Sprinkle with the pretzel salt
  24. Bake until golden brown and cooked through, about 15 minutes

White Bread

from allrecipes.com

Makes 2 loaves

Ingredients

  • 2 cups warm water
  • 2/3 cup white sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 6 cups bread flour

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, dissolve the sugar in warm water, then stir in yeast. Allow to proof.
  2. Mix salt and oil into the yeast.
  3. Mix in flour one cup at a time
  4. Knead dough on a lightly-floured surface until smooth
  5. Place in a well-oiled bowl and turn dough to coat. Cover and let rise until doubled, about 1 hour.
  6. Punch dough down and kneed for a few minutes.
  7. Divide dough in half, shape into loaves, and place into two well-oiled 9x5 inch loaf pans.
  8. Allow to rise for 30 minutes or until dough has risen 1 inch above pans.
  9. Bake at 350 degrees for 30 minutes
