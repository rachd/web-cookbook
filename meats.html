Asian Grilled Chicken

Ingredients

  • 1 chicken, separated
  • 1/8 cup honey
  • 1/8 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/4 teaspoon ginger

Directions

  1. Whisk together all ingredients except the chicken.
  2. Pour marinade over chicken and let sit for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Grill the chicken over medium heat for about 30 minutes or until cooked.

Chicken Pot Pie

Makes 2 pies

Ingredients

  • pie crusts for two 9-inch pies
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1 cup chopped carrot
  • 1 cup chopped potato
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup half and half
  • 2 cups chicken, cooked, chopped
  • 1 cup peas
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375.
  2. In large frying pan, saute celery, carrots, and potato in the butter.
  3. Add flour and stir until smooth. Cook one minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Add chicken broth and half and half, stirring until thickened and bubbly.
  5. Stir in chicken, peas, salt, and pepper. Allow to cool.
  6. Roll out pie crusts and place bottom crust in pan.
  7. Pour in filling and place top crust. Fold over edges and crimp.
  8. Poke slits in top and brush with milk
  9. Bake uncovered for thirty minutes then cover with foil and bake 30 minutes
  10. Cool 10 minutes before eating

Crab Cakes

from finecooking.com

Makes 8 crab cakes

Ingredients

  • 1 pound jumbo lump crabmeat
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup mayonnnaise
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons Old Bay seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 1/4 cups fresh breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olice oil
  • lemon wedges for serving

Directions

  1. Drain the crabmeat and set aside in a medium mixing bowl
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together egg, mayonnaise, mustard, Old Bat, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, and salt
  3. Scrape mixture over crab and mix gently until well combined, gently breaking up lumps
  4. Sprinkle breadcrumbs and parsley over the mixture, mixing in thoroughly but gently
  5. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 to 3 hours
  6. Shape crab mixture into 8 cakes about 1-inch thick
  7. In a 12-inch nonstick skillet, heat the butter and olive oil over medium heat
  8. When butter is frothy, add cakes
  9. Cook until dark golden brown on underside, about 4 minutes
  10. Flip cakes, reduce heat to medium low, and continue cooking until other side is browned, 4-5 minutes
  11. Serve with lemon wedges

Kung Pao Chicken

from seriouseats.com

serves 4

Ingredients

    For the chicken
  • 1.5 pounds bonless skinless chicken thighs, cut into 3/4-inch chunks
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch
    • For the stir-fry sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon distilled white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon roasted sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 large bell peppers, cut into 3/4-inch dice
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh garlic (about 2 medium cloves)
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh ginger
  • 1 scallion, white and light green parts only, finely minced
  • 8 small dried red Chinese or Arbol chilis

Directions

  1. Combine chicken, salt, pepper, soy sauce, wine sugar, sesame oil, and cornstarch in a medium bowl and toss to coat. Set aside for 20 minutes.
  2. Combine soy sauce, wine, vinegar, chicken stock, sugar, sesame oil, and cornstarch in a small bowl and whisk together until homogenous. Set aside.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a wok over high heat until smoking.
  4. Add chicken, spread into a single layer, and cook without moving until lightly browned, about 1 minute.
  5. Continue cooking, tossing and stirring frequently, until the exterior is opaque but chicken is still slightly raw in the center, about 2 minutes longer.
  6. Transfer chicken to a clean bowl and set aside.
  7. Wipe out wok and heat remaining 2 tablespoons oil over high heat until smoking.
  8. Add bell pepper and celery and cook, stirring and tossing occasionally, until brightly colored and browned in spots, about 1 minute.
  9. Add peanuts and toss to combine.
  10. Push vegetables up side of wok to clear a space in the center.
  11. Add garlic ginger, scallions, and dried chilies and cook, stirring, until fregrant, about 30 seconds.
  12. Return chicken to wok and toss to combine.
  13. Stir sauce and add to wok.
  14. Cook, tossing, until sauce thickens and coats ingredients and chicken is cooked through, about 1 minute longer.
  15. Serve immediately.

Rosemary-Sage Pork Chops

from 918plate.com

Ingredients

  • pork chops
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon fresh rosemary, chopped finely
  • 1/4 teaspoon fresh sage, chopped finely
  • salt and pepper

Directions

  1. Combine oil, rosemary, and sage
  2. Pat pork chops dry
  3. Rub pork chops with oil mixture. Salt and pepper both sides generously.
  4. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat
  5. Sear pork until cooked through, turning every 2 minutes
  6. Let sit for 5 minutes before serving

Seared Tuna Steak with Avocado and Cilantro-Lime Dressing

from myhusbandhatesveggies.com

Serves 1

Ingredients

  • 1 fresh tuna steak
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  • 2 cloves freshly grated garlic
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • juice of 2 limes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 handful chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 haas avocado, cubed
  • salt and pepper

Directions

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, ginger, garlic, brown sugar, and lime juice
  2. Slowly drizzle in oil, whisking constantly, until incorporated
  3. Add cilantro and mix gently
  4. Set a dry non-stick pan over medium-high heat until hot
  5. Season tuna steak on one side with plenty of salt and pepper
  6. Place tuna, seasoned side down, into the pan
  7. Season the side facing up
  8. Let tuna cook for a minute then flip
  9. Spoon a large spoonful of dressing over cooked side
  10. Cook for one more minute
  11. Remove tuna from pan
  12. Top with cubed avocade and drizzle with remaining dressing
