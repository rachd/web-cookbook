Lasagna

makes 12-15 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 1-lb package lasagna, uncooked (16 noodles)
  • 1 16-oz container ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 lb bulk Italian sausage, cooked and drained, out of casing
  • 2 jars tomato and basil pasta sauce
  • 4 cups (1 lb) shredded mozzarella cheese

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Cook lasagna noodles as package directs
  3. In bowl, mix ricotta, Parmesan, and eggs well
  4. Grease bottom of 13x9 inch pan
  5. Spread 1/2 cup pasta sauce
  6. Top with 4 noodles, 1/3 ricotta mix, 1/3 sausage, 1/3 pasta sauce, 1/3 mozzarella
  7. Repeat layering twice, ending with noodles, sauce, mozzarella
  8. Cover and bake 1 hour
  9. Uncover and let stand 15 minutes before serving

Stuffed Shells

Serves 4

from From Our House to Yours

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound spinach leaves, stems removed
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup whole-milk ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 pound whole-milk mozzarella cheese, coarsely grated
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 tablespoon finely minced fresh basil
  • 20 conchiglione (jumbo shells)
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated pecorino romano cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees
  2. Lightly oil a baking dish large enough to hold the shells in one layer
  3. Wash the spinach carefully in cold water
  4. Transfer to a medium skillet with just the water clinging to the leaves
  5. Cover and cook over medium heat until the leaves are just wilted, about 3 minutes, turning once or twice
  6. Transfer to a sieve and place under cold running water until cool
  7. Drain well and squeeze dry
  8. Chop spinach finely
  9. In a medium bowl, combine spinach, garlic, ricotta, mozzarella, egg, basil, salt, and pepper
  10. Cook the shells in a large pot of boiling water until not quite al dente
  11. Drain the shells and transfer them to a bowl
  12. Toss with the olive oil to keep them from sticking
  13. Put half the tomato sauce on the bottom of the prepared dish
  14. Fill each shell with a heaping tablespoon of the stuffing
  15. Arrange the shells in a single layer in the dish
  16. Spoon the remaining sauce over and around the shells
  17. Top with pecorino romano cheese
  18. Cover with aluminum foil and bake until bubbling hot, 30-40 minutes
